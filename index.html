Baber of the Woods

A collection of photography of some of the finest specimens of fungi
I have come across in my forays through the wilds of Northeast Ohio and elsewhere...

A young, prime fruiting of gem studded puffball. Edible with caution.
Latin Name - Lycoperdon perlatum

A very fresh, young chicken of the woods, choice edible.
Latin Name - Laetiporus Sulphureus

An unidentified specimen, likely from the coprinus genus, edibility unknown.
Latin Name - ??????

A large fruiting of coral tooth fungus, grows almost exclusively on downed beech, as seen here, edible.
Latin Name - Hericium Coralloides

One of my favorite photos, non-native golden oysters. All credit of the find goes to my dog Rudy, choice edible.
Latin Name - Pleurotus citrinopileatus

Common Oyster Mushroom, commonly fruits up standing dead hardwoods, choice edible.
Latin Name - Pleurotus Ostreatus

Another image of a slightly older chicken of the woods, basking in morning sun, choice edible.
Latin Name - Laetiporus Sulphureus

Dryad's saddle mushroom, grows quite large and is quite common in early spring, edible but not desirable.
Latin Name - Polyporus Squamosus

Obviously not mushrooms, but a mixed crate of spring edible, japanese knotweed and ostrich fern fiddleheads.

Late spring fruiting of red reishi on hemlock, Edible, but as medicinal in form of tea/tincture.
Latin Name - Ganoderma Tsugae

Young jackolantern mushroom, a very beautiful species to observe, gill supposedly bioluminescent at night, poisonus, but not known to be lethal.
Latin Name - Omphalotus olearius

A small fruiting of bear's head tooth fungus, choice edible.
Latin Name - Hericium Americanum

Another mixed crate, including more hericium, lilac boletes, chantrelles, maitake and some paw paws.

A beautiful young fruiting of caesar's mushroom. Edible, but with extreme caution, it is a member of the highly toxic amanita family and must be certain of identification.
Latin Name - Amanita caesarea

A line-up of some beautiful spring morels, choice edible.
Latin Name - Morchella Esculenta
